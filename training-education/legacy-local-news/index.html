Legacy local news publishers

Trinity Mirror has been UK’s largest regional news publisher since buying Local World (126 titles) in October 2015:

Source: Monopolising Local News (Kings College London, 2016)

The five largest publishers now control nearly 80% of the UKs local and regional titles:

  • Trinity Mirror (1903, has been Reach plc since May 2018)

    • already had Daily/Sunday Mirror, Sunday People, Scottish Daily Record, Sunday Mail
    • bought Northern & Shell (inc. Daily/Sunday Express, Daily/Sunday Star) Feb 2018
    • printer (inc. Guardian/Observer)
    • recent closure of Exeter/Truro offices means now only Plymouth in SW

  • Johnstone (1767)

    • i, Scotsman/Edinburgh Evening News, Yorkshire/Evening Post
    • entered administration Nov 2018 after failing to find buyer
    • JPIMedia continue to publish ~200 titles but future uncertain
    • Reach & Newsquest competed to buy Nov 2019 then dropped out
    • DMGT (Daily Mail/on Sunday, Metro) bought i for £49.6m Nov 2019
    • National World (ex-Local World cost cutter David Montgomery) bid?

  • Newsquest (1995 US private equity buyout of Reed Regional Newspapers)

    • Oxford Mail, Brighton Argus, Glasgow Herald, The National (Scotland)
    • owned by Gannett, taken over by GateHouse Media Nov 2019
    • group now largest US newspaper publisher (~1000 titles)
    • neither Johnstone nor Newsquest own any Devon titles
    • printer

  • Tindle (1947 Ray Tindle, owner, manager to 2017 (age 90), now son Owen)

    • ~150 titles, strictly local, 14 SW brands = 28 titles
    • “local journalism is a brand with a future” Feb 2018

  • Archant (1845 Jeremiah Colman et al)

    • Archant Life, East Anglia + London inc. Ham & High, The New European
    • closed Norwich presses, no longer printing own titles Nov 2019

Consequences of consolidation:

  • since 1985, more local public interest news articles have come from managed media sources than reporters attending council or public meetings - since 1995, fewer than 10%
  • more than half of UK parliamentary constituencies and nearly two thirds of UK local authority districts are no longer served by a daily local newspaper
  • 96% of local authority districts have a dominant daily and weekly local newspaper publisher
  • 206 parliamentary constituencies that have no daily local newspaper received five or fewer mentions in the national press during the 2015 general election campaign, and 50 of these were not mentioned at all

Exeter area independent local newspapers:

  • South Molton & District News

    • monthly A4 glossy, 1500 copies, £36pa postal subscription, no website
    • local newsagents/village stores/post offices/petrol stations (£1)
    • “unrivalled coverage of South Molton Town Council meetings”

  • The Moorlander (launched 2016 by ex-Fleet Street war photographer)

    • fortnightly 40 page tabloid, 5000 copies, website
    • 450 distribution points + direct delivery (Fridays 50p where sold)
    • expanded as Cranbrook News in 2017, since folded

    • folded May 2019

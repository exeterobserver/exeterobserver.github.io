    Information sources

    A guide to help potential contributors develop their skills.

    If you are considering contributing to Exeter Observer please see our contributors guide.

    Information source types

    • Importance of distinguishing between source types (even though distinctions artificial, overlapping, heuristic)

    • Primary, secondary, tertiary (often used by academics cf. Princeton University library guide)
    • Original, derivative, meta

    • Reliable, subjective/partial/biased, fake/misleading/misinformation

    • Personal contacts, media gatekeepers, politicians etc are also important sources (cf. perspective sources).

    cf. Local government transparency & information access rights.
